About Me

I’m Dhairya Parikh, a Computer Engineering graduate student at Virginia Tech, deeply passionate about cybersecurity, machine learning, and secure network design. My work focuses on bridging the gap between academic research and real-world cybersecurity challenges.

I’ve developed reinforcement learning-based fuzzers for 5G/O-RAN testing, designed automated security frameworks, and built tools for protocol validation and vulnerability detection. At the Commonwealth Cyber Initiative (CCI), I contributed to ORAN security research, while also collaborating with Booz Allen on advanced ML applications in security.

My experience spans OPC UA protocol engineering, PKI infrastructure, SIEM integration, and ML-driven anomaly detection across both industrial and academic environments.

I’m always excited to contribute to innovative security research and collaborate on AI-powered solutions for modern communication networks.
